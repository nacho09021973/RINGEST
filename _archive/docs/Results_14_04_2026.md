## Results

We applied two independent bulk diagnostics to the unified cohort of 82 ringdown events. First, the bulk-equation classifier assigns all 82 events to the **Einstein + matter** category, with no events classified as compatible with pure Einstein vacuum and none flagged as non-Einstein or deformed. The mean Einstein score is 0.53, indicating partial but nontrivial consistency with an Einstein-like sector, while disfavoring a pure-vacuum interpretation.

Second, the geometry-physics contract is satisfied across the full cohort under relaxed inference semantics. Of the 82 events, 74 pass the contract under strict criteria, while the remaining 8 pass under a relaxed criterion associated exclusively with a soft failure in the `correlator_structure` sub-contract. Importantly, those 8 events do **not** violate regularity, causality, or unitarity, and no real failures are observed anywhere in the cohort.

Taken together, these two diagnostics support a coherent picture: within the scope of the present tests, the cohort is uniformly compatible with a healthy effective bulk sector of **Einstein + matter** type, with no evidence for a separate pathological or strongly deformed geometric class.

## Discussion

The most conservative interpretation of these results is that the 82-event cohort does not split into two distinct geometric populations. Instead, the data favor a single effective bulk sector that remains structurally well behaved throughout the sample. The distinction between the 74 strict events and the 8 relaxed events appears to be localized in the correlator channel alone, rather than in the hard geometric consistency conditions.

This point matters. If the relaxed subset had also shown failures in regularity, causality, or unitarity, one could reasonably argue for a second geometric regime or for a genuinely pathological subset. That is not what is observed. The relaxed events preserve all hard geometric constraints and differ only through a soft correlator warning. Within the present evidence, this is more naturally interpreted as a degradation of correlator identifiability or quality, rather than as a distinct bulk class.

At the same time, the uniform `82/82` assignment to the Einstein + matter class should not be overinterpreted. It is consistent with a non-vacuum effective bulk sector, but it does not by itself identify the microscopic origin of the effective matter content, nor does it exclude the possibility that the classifier is only weakly discriminative across the current cohort. For that reason, the correct statement is that the cohort is **compatible with** an Einstein-plus-effective-matter interpretation, not that such an interpretation has been uniquely established.

The physically relevant message is therefore limited but meaningful: the ringdown cohort, when embedded in the reconstructed bulk sector used here, is not described as pure Einstein vacuum, yet it also shows no evidence of hard geometric inconsistency. The minority relaxed subset does not presently force a two-class interpretation; instead, it marks a localized tension in the correlator channel that should be treated as a follow-up target rather than as evidence for a distinct phase.
